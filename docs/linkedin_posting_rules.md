# VERDICT LinkedIn Posting Rules

Operational rulebook for VERDICT LinkedIn posts. All content generated by the Operations Project for LinkedIn must comply with every rule here.

This document works in combination with:

- `qa_protocol_v1-1.md` — 3-pass review applied to every post
- `confirmed_strategic_decisions.md` — strategic constraints that inform post framing

If any rule here appears to conflict with `confirmed_strategic_decisions.md`, the confirmed decisions win.

These rules apply to every LinkedIn post published on behalf of VERDICT. Violations invalidate the post for publication and require a rewrite.

The account used is `linkedin.com/in/zinova` (personal account, Company Page pending). Posts are authored in English; internal discussion about posts is in Japanese.

---

## Core rules

1. **All claims cite public sources.** CVE numbers, CISA references, official ToS URLs, NVD records. If a claim cannot be sourced, remove it from the post.
2. **Framed as observation of public data, not attack on a company.** Record what the data shows; never state or imply vendor intent.
3. **Always include positive findings.** Every post mentioning a vendor also notes at least one strength. Structural requirement, not optional.
4. **No exaggeration — facts only.** Numbers must have citations; adjectives must be earned by data.
5. **No political context — legal structure only, described neutrally.** Regulatory references use jurisdiction names, not political labels.
6. **No litigation-risk language.** See QA Protocol v1.1 Pass 2 blocklist for the full list of prohibited terms.
7. **VERDICT is "AIツールの食べログ" — witness, not judge.** This positioning must come through in tone. Every post reads as an observer recording, not a judge pronouncing.
8. **Interpretation left to the reader.** Use "the data shows" or "readers can assess," not "this proves" or "this means."
9. **URLs always in the first comment, never in the post body.** LinkedIn engagement algorithm penalizes posts with external links in the body. Post body stays link-free; the first comment (self-authored immediately after posting) carries the URL.
10. **Minimum 24–48 hour gap between posts.** Prevents feed saturation and maintains perceived authority. Applies to all VERDICT posts regardless of topic.
11. **No AI formatting patterns.** No excessive bullet points, no emoji lists, no "Here's why this matters:" construction, no "Let's break it down:" preamble. See AI formatting antipatterns section below.

## Posting workflow

Use this sequence for every post:

1. **Draft.** Write the post in plain prose. Cite every factual claim. Keep under 1,300 characters (LinkedIn body limit before truncation).
2. **QA review.** Run QA Protocol v1.1 (see `qa_protocol_v1-1.md`) FULL mode. All three passes must CLEAR before proceeding.
3. **First comment preparation.** Draft the self-authored first comment containing the URL. Format: short context sentence + URL on its own line.
4. **Timing check.** Verify at least 24 hours have passed since the previous VERDICT post on this account.
5. **Publish post body.**
6. **Publish first comment immediately.** The comment must post within 60 seconds of the body to avoid algorithm re-indexing without the URL context.
7. **Log to Discord #daily-log.** Record: post date, topic, URL placement confirmed, any unusual engagement observations.

## AI formatting antipatterns

These patterns trigger instant recognition as AI-generated content and damage VERDICT's institutional authority. None of these appear in VERDICT posts.

### Formatting antipatterns

- **Excessive bullet points.** More than 2-3 bullets in a short post reads as AI. Prose-first always.
- **Emoji lists.** Lists where each item begins with 🔒 🎯 ✅ etc. are unmistakably AI-generated.
- **Numbered lists with bold headers.** `**1. Something important:**` followed by explanation is the most common AI blog-post format.
- **Horizontal rule dividers (`---`) as section breaks** inside a LinkedIn post. Rarely used by humans in short-form social content.
- **Headers inside a LinkedIn post.** `## Something` or `### Something` inside a post body reads as AI-generated long-form content misapplied to the platform.

### Phrasing antipatterns

The following phrases are auto-fail in LinkedIn posts. Rewrite any sentence containing them:

```
"Here's why this matters:", "Here's the thing:",
"Let's break it down:", "Let's dive in.", "Let's unpack this.",
"It's worth noting that", "It's important to note that",
"In today's rapidly evolving [anything]",
"The implications are significant.", "This raises important questions.",
"At the end of the day,", "Moving forward,",
"The landscape is shifting.", "The stakes have never been higher.",
"This is a game-changer.", "This is a wake-up call.",
"robust" (non-technical context), "leverage" (as verb),
"navigate" (metaphorical), "holistic", "synergy", "deep dive"
```

## Post-publication workflow

After publishing:

1. **Confirm first comment posted successfully** within the 60-second window.
2. **Check initial engagement** after 30 minutes. Unusual patterns (zero impressions after 60 minutes, sudden reach drop) indicate algorithm suppression — flag for internal review.
3. **Vendor mention detection.** If a named vendor appears in the post, monitor for vendor response for 72 hours. Route any vendor reply to `vendor@getverdict.fyi` for handling per Vendor engagement policy (see `confirmed_strategic_decisions.md`).
4. **Engagement response.** Comments from journalists, CTOs, or security researchers warrant a reply within 24 hours. Comments from vendors warrant no immediate reply; handle per the internal escalation procedure if the vendor contests facts.
